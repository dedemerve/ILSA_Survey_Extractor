01   Abstract

What this survey does

"The first systematic map of how AI and machine learning are being applied to international large-scale assessment data, and where the field stands methodologically."

International Large-Scale Assessments have generated rich microdata across PISA, TIMSS, PIRLS, TALIS, ICCS, ICILS, and PIAAC. Over the past decade, a growing body of studies has applied artificial intelligence and machine learning methods to these datasets for purposes such as predicting student outcomes, profiling learners, scoring open-ended items, and analyzing behavioral process data.

Yet no prior work has systematically reviewed this literature across all major ILSA programs, evaluated methodological rigor, or produced an openly accessible structured evidence base. This survey fills that gap through a PRISMA-guided review of 130 peer-reviewed studies, producing a fully structured open dataset alongside the synthesis.

Motivation

Fragmented evidence scattered across programs, methods, and countries makes it difficult to assess the state of the field or identify methodological gaps.

gap analysis
Approach

PRISMA-guided systematic search, dual screening, structured information extraction across 9 field categories, controlled vocabulary taxonomy.

survey paper
Output

130 coded studies · 202 main findings · 1,907 predictor records · open dataset under CC BY 4.0.

02   Methodology

Study selection & screening process

RQ 1
How are AI and machine learning methods applied in international large-scale assessment research, and what methodological practices characterize this body of work?
RQ 2
Which ILSA programs are most studied, and what ML algorithm families dominate? How do studies handle plausible values and sampling weights?
RQ 3
What outcome domains and predictor categories appear most frequently, and where do critical evidence gaps remain for future research?
874
Step 01: Identification
Database search
Full-text and keyword search across Web of Science, Scopus, ERIC, and Google Scholar using terms combining ILSA program names with AI/ML methodology keywords.
412
Step 02: Deduplication and Screening
Title & abstract review
Duplicate records removed; remaining studies screened on title and abstract by two independent reviewers. Disagreements resolved by consensus.
198
Step 03: Full-text eligibility
Full-text assessment
Full texts retrieved and assessed against inclusion criteria: peer-reviewed empirical studies applying AI/ML to ILSA microdata, published 2020–April 2026.
130
Step 04: Final corpus
Included in synthesis
130 studies included in final systematic synthesis and structured extraction. Each study coded across 9 field categories using a pre-registered schema.
Inclusion criteria
  • Peer-reviewed journal articles or conference papers
  • Applies at least one AI/ML method
  • Uses ILSA microdata (PISA, TIMSS, PIRLS, TALIS, ICCS, ICILS, PIAAC)
  • Published 2020–April 2026
  • English-language publication
Exclusion criteria
  • Non-ILSA datasets or aggregated country-level only
  • Purely theoretical or methodological framework papers
  • Traditional statistics only (HLM, SEM, OLS) without ML
  • National or technical report documents
  • Mobile learning apps or tool development without ILSA data
Extraction fields (9 categories)
  • ML technique family and primary algorithm
  • Plausible value handling protocol
  • Sampling weight usage
  • Target outcome domain
  • Predictor variables with level coding
Quality indicators
  • PV handling documented (yes / no / partial)
  • Sampling weights applied
  • Model performance metrics reported
  • Sample size and country coverage stated
  • Replication feasibility assessment

06   Limitations

Known constraints

English-language restriction

The search was restricted to English-language publications. Studies published in Turkish, Chinese, Spanish, or other languages may represent a non-trivial share of the ILSA-ML literature and are not captured.

Search date cutoff

The search covers publications from 2020 to April 2026. Studies published after this date are not included; the field is evolving rapidly and the landscape will continue to shift.

Reporting incompleteness

Many studies do not clearly state their plausible value protocol or sampling weight decisions. Classification for these studies relies on textual inference, introducing some uncertainty into the rigor coding.

Extraction granularity

The extraction schema captures the primary ML method but does not code all hyperparameter choices, ensemble configurations, or secondary analytical decisions reported in the original papers.

Future directions
→
Multilingual extension of the systematic search to capture non-English ILSA-ML studies
→
Statistical meta-analysis of effect sizes and predictor importance for common outcome-domain pairs
→
Formal replication study applying correct PV and weight protocols to published ML analyses
→
Causal inference methods, a rapidly growing subfield not fully captured in the current corpus
→
Process data and log-file applications as a dedicated sub-review, given growth in this area since PISA 2022
